# 12. Key Risks & Mitigations

---

## Risk Register

### Risk 1: Meta API Policy Change
**Level: HIGH**

**Description:** SWIPE's core value is IG-native automation. Meta has historically changed API access rules with little notice — reducing message volumes, restricting automation types, or requiring re-approval. A policy shift could limit auto-send functionality or require expensive re-architecture.

**Mitigations:**
- Build WhatsApp Business API integration in parallel (same Meta ecosystem but more stable for B2B, different policy track)
- Architect SWIPE so the DM channel is a replaceable module — the CRM, pipeline, and order management value exists independently of the DM automation
- Pursue Meta Business Partner status for advance policy consultation access
- Maintain email fallback channel for all buyer communication
- Monitor Meta's developer changelog weekly; subscribe to Graph API release notes

**Contingency:** If IG DM automation becomes severely restricted, pivot messaging to "Wholesale CRM with IG + WhatsApp + Email" — the pipeline and order management features still deliver value.

---

### Risk 2: Low Brand Tech Literacy
**Level: MEDIUM**

**Description:** Target ICP (indie brand founders, 1–3 person teams) may struggle with OAuth flows, webhook concepts, and API connections. Drop-off during onboarding is a primary conversion risk. If brands can't get connected, they never see the value.

**Mitigations:**
- Design onboarding as a guided wizard: "Connect Instagram → Connect Shopify → Import buyers → Done" with no technical jargon
- Include 30-minute live onboarding call for all Growth+ customers (recorded for async)
- Video walkthroughs for every connection step (Loom-style, embedded in app)
- Auto-detect common errors (e.g., IG account not set to Business) and provide plain-English fix instructions
- Reduce required steps: Shopify connection optional for MVP (can use SWIPE without Shopify for first 7 days)
- Hire a part-time customer success person by Month 6

---

### Risk 3: Faire Counter-Move
**Level: MEDIUM**

**Description:** Faire could build IG-native outreach tools, acquire a DM automation competitor, or launch a "Faire Outreach" product targeting the same workflow SWIPE addresses.

**Mitigations:**
- Focus on the structural advantage Faire cannot replicate without changing their business model: **zero commission** and **brand owns the buyer relationship**
- Build the reorder engine and buyer scoring features that Faire has no incentive to build (they benefit from brands being acquisition-dependent)
- Maintain a "SWIPE + Faire" narrative — SWIPE is for managing buyers you already have; Faire is for finding new ones. Position as complementary.
- Faire Direct import feature (Scale tier) makes SWIPE a better home for Faire-sourced relationships, not a threat to Faire's marketplace

---

### Risk 4: AI Message Quality
**Level: MEDIUM**

**Description:** Poor AI-drafted messages sent to buyers could damage brand relationships, sound robotic, or mis-represent the brand's voice. A single bad AI message to an important buyer could cause churn.

**Mitigations:**
- **Starter tier default: human-in-loop only.** No AI message is ever auto-sent without brand approval on the entry tier.
- Auto-send only unlocked in Growth tier after 30 days of usage data establishes baseline quality
- Confidence thresholds always visible in the UI — brand can see why a message was or wasn't auto-sent
- "Reject + Retrain" flow: when brand rejects an AI draft, they explain why → this data improves future drafts
- Brand voice training in Scale tier: model learns from approved messages specifically from that brand
- Always show the AI-drafted text with a clear "This was generated by AI — edit before sending" warning

---

### Risk 5: Meta Business Partner Access
**Level: HIGH**

**Description:** Sending DMs at any meaningful volume through the official Instagram API requires Meta Business Partner status or a white-label arrangement with an existing MBP. Meta's approval process is slow (1–3 months) and not guaranteed.

**Mitigations:**
- **Start the MBP application immediately** — this has the longest lead time of any pre-launch task
- **Interim option:** Negotiate a white-label integration with an existing Meta Business Partner (e.g., build on top of ManyChat's API or a similar platform that already has MBP status)
- **Limit beta to low-volume brands** (< 50 DMs/day per brand) during the MBP approval period — this stays within standard API limits available to all Business account developers
- Document the MBP pathway in the pitch deck: investors and enterprise customers will ask

---

### Risk 6: Shopify Dependency
**Level: LOW-MEDIUM**

**Description:** SWIPE requires Shopify for order management in v1. If a brand uses WooCommerce, BigCommerce, or a custom platform, they can't use SWIPE fully.

**Mitigations:**
- Accept this as an intentional constraint for v1 — Shopify has ~30% of the e-commerce platform market and the ICP (indie fashion/CPG brands) over-indexes heavily toward Shopify
- Design the order management module to be platform-agnostic in the data model (order records don't have Shopify-specific fields baked in)
- Add WooCommerce integration in Phase 3 based on demand signals from the customer base
- For brands without Shopify: v1 can still deliver value via CRM + DM automation + manual invoice link sending (Stripe link) — Shopify is required only for the draft order automation

---

### Risk 7: Pricing Pressure / Race to Bottom
**Level: LOW**

**Description:** A well-funded competitor (or ManyChat with a new product line) could enter the wholesale automation space and compete on price.

**Mitigations:**
- Build switching costs through data: the longer a brand uses SWIPE, the richer their buyer profiles, order history, and trained AI voice — this data doesn't transfer to a competitor
- Prioritize NPS and customer relationships over growth velocity — brands that trust you don't switch for $20/mo savings
- The Agency tier creates structural stickiness: a VA managing 10 brands on SWIPE won't switch all their clients for a marginal price difference
